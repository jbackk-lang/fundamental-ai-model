# Semantics — Interpretation of Structures and Transitions  
*Fundamental AI Model*

## 1. Overview
Semantics defines **what the structures mean** in the Fundamental AI Model.  
Geometry, logic and operators describe *how* the system behaves.  
Semantics describes *why* these behaviors matter and *what they represent*.

The model uses topological transitions to encode meaning.

---

## 2. Semantic Units

### 2.1 Determination (D)
**Meaning:**  
A stable, interpretable, oriented state of information.

Semantically:
- structure is readable  
- orientation is defined  
- information is accessible  
- context is preserved  

Equivalent to “meaningful state”.

---

### 2.2 Indetermination (I)
**Meaning:**  
A potential, ambiguous, orientation‑flipped state.

Semantically:
- structure is unresolved  
- meaning is latent  
- context is unstable  
- interpretation is deferred  

Equivalent to “pre‑meaning” or “unresolved meaning”.

---

### 2.3 Transition (T)
**Meaning:**  
The moment when meaning changes.

Semantically:
- interpretation shifts  
- structure reorients  
- context updates  
- time is generated  

Equivalent to “semantic event”.

---

## 3. Collapse Semantics

### 3.1 Collapse
**Meaning:**  
Loss of interpretability.

Collapse occurs when:
- structure becomes undefined  
- meaning cannot be preserved  
- orientation fails  

### 3.2 Residue
**Meaning:**  
Irrecoverable semantic fragments.

Examples:
- noise  
- ambiguity  
- lost context  
- irreversible decay  

Residue is the **semantic memory of collapse**.

---

## 4. Horizon Semantics

### 4.1 Horizon
**Meaning:**  
The boundary where meaning cannot return.

Semantically:
- interpretation stops  
- context cannot be restored  
- information becomes unreachable  

### 4.2 Eye
**Meaning:**  
The point where meaning flips.

Semantically:
- the origin of new interpretation  
- the generator of semantic direction  
- the moment of irreversible change  

The Eye is the **semantic pivot**.

---

## 5. Chain Semantics

### 5.1 Link
**Meaning:**  
One complete semantic cycle.

A link represents:
- one interpretation  
- one collapse  
- one residue layer  
- one unit of semantic time  

### 5.2 Chain
**Meaning:**  
History of meaning.

The chain is:
- cumulative  
- irreversible  
- directional  
- context‑building  

Semantic time = length of the chain.
