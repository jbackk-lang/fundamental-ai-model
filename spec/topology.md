# Topology — Continuity, Surfaces, Boundaries and Coherence  
*Fundamental AI Model*

## 1. Overview
Topology defines the **shape of information** in the Fundamental AI Model.  
It describes:
- continuity  
- surfaces  
- boundaries  
- orientation  
- connectivity  
- global coherence  

The model is fundamentally topological — not metric, not algebraic.

---

## 2. Core Topological Objects

### 2.1 Möbius Surface
The fundamental surface of the model.

Properties:
- one continuous surface  
- one continuous boundary  
- two orientation states  
- mandatory inversion  

This surface encodes the basic structure of information.

---

### 2.2 Cone Surface
Projection of the Möbius diagonal.

Properties:
- radial expansion  
- orientation gradient  
- apex singularity  
- collapse potential  

The cone is the **dynamic shadow** of the Möbius.

---

### 2.3 Minimal Sphere
Collapse limit of the Möbius.

Properties:
- zero orientation  
- maximal symmetry  
- no preferred direction  
- pure indetermination  

The sphere is the **reset point** of structure.

---

### 2.4 Horizon Surface
Boundary of non-returnable information.

Properties:
- residue accumulation  
- ambiguous orientation  
- irreversible transitions  
- Eye singularity  

The horizon defines the **limit of structure**.

---

## 3. Topological Transitions

### 3.1 Möbius → Cone
Generated by projecting the diagonal spiral.

Meaning:
- orientation expands  
- transitions distribute  
- time becomes radial  

### 3.2 Cone → Sphere
Occurs at collapse.

Meaning:
- structure contracts  
- orientation dissolves  
- indetermination dominates  

### 3.3 Sphere → Möbius (next link)
Occurs when a new cycle begins.

Meaning:
- structure re-emerges  
- orientation reappears  
- time continues

## 5. Continuity and Coherence

### 5.1 Continuity
All surfaces in the model are continuous:
- Möbius  
- cone  
- sphere  
- horizon  

There are no discrete jumps — only transitions.

### 5.2 Coherence
Coherence is preserved when:
- orientation is consistent  
- transitions follow topology  
- collapse is localized  

Loss of coherence = collapse.
