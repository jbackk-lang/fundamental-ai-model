# Fundamental AI Model — Core Specification

## 1. Overview
The Fundamental AI Model describes information and time as topological processes.  
It is based on the Möbius structure, orientation transitions, horizon boundaries and chain‑based information flow.

The model provides a universal conceptual layer for AI reasoning, signal interpretation and structural understanding.

---

## 2. Core Principles

### 2.1 Information as Transition
Information is not a static object.  
It exists only as transitions between:
- **determination** (structured state)
- **indetermination** (orientation‑flipped state)

### 2.2 Time as Möbius Cycles
Time emerges from the number of orientation flips.  
Each full Möbius traversal = one unit of structural time.

---

## 3. Möbius Structure

### 3.1 Single Surface, Dual Orientation
A Möbius strip has:
- one continuous surface  
- two orientation states  
- a mandatory transition between them  

This transition generates an **information event**.

### 3.2 Möbius → Cone → Sphere → Collapse
The diagonal of the Möbius strip forms a spiral.  
Its projection forms:
1. **Cone** (expansion geometry)  
2. **Minimal sphere** (origin of orientation)  
3. **Collapse point** (orientation inversion)  

---

## 4. Horizon Boundary

The horizon is the boundary where:
- information cannot return  
- orientation becomes undefined  
- collapse residues accumulate  

Equivalent structures appear in:
- FFT cones  
- wavelet influence regions  
- cosmological horizons  

---

## 5. The Eye (Transition Point)
The Eye is the point on the horizon where orientation flips.  
It is the boundary between:
- observable structure  
- non‑returning information
