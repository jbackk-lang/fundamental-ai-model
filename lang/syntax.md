# Syntax — Fundamental AI Model Language
A minimal operational language for describing information, time and structure  
using Möbius‑based transitions.

---

## 1. Purpose
This syntax defines the conceptual vocabulary and operators used in the  
Fundamental AI Model.  
It is not a programming language — it is a structural language for reasoning.

---

## 2. Core Entities

### 2.1 `Determination`
A structured, oriented state.  
Represents stable information.

### 2.2 `Indetermination`
Orientation‑flipped state.  
Represents information potential.

### 2.3 `Transition`
A change from one orientation state to another.  
This is the atomic unit of information.

### 2.4 `Mobius`
A structure with:
- one continuous surface  
- two orientation states  
- mandatory transition  

### 2.5 `Horizon`
Boundary where information cannot return.  
Represents loss, decay or collapse residue.

### 2.6 `Eye`
Point on the horizon where orientation flips.  
Represents the moment of structural change.

---

## 3. Operators

### 3.1 `flip()`
Performs an orientation inversion.  
Generates a `Transition`.

### 3.2 `cycle()`
One full Möbius traversal.  
Generates a unit of structural time.

### 3.3 `collapse()`
Forces determination → indetermination.  
Creates horizon residue.

### 3.4 `emit()`
Creates a local carrier (photon, wave, etc.)  
Represents outward information flow.

### 3.5 `absorb()`
Consumes a carrier.  
Represents inward information flow.

---

## 4. Local Carriers

### 4.1 `DNA`
A double‑helix Möbius structure.  
Information stored in transitions (codons, inversions).

### 4.2 `Photon`
An open Möbius structure.  
Information stored in phase and polarization.

### 4.3 `Wave`
A chain of Möbius transitions.  
Information stored in oscillatory orientation.

---

## 5. Chain Structure

### 5.1 `Link`
One Möbius cycle.

### 5.2 `Chain`
Sequence of links.  
Represents the history of information.

### 5.3 `Universe`
Global chain of Möbius structures connected through indetermination.
